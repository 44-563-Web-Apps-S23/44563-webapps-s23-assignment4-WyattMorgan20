Wyatt Morgan Snips a Play

The Last Straw

By: Bosworth Crocker

KARL.

[_Striking at_ FRITZI.]

You're as big a tough as Henny Nies.

 FRITZI.

[_Proud of this alleged likeness._]

I'm going to be a man just like my father; I'll holler and make them stand around.
KARL.

[_With conviction._]

What you need is a good licking.

[_Telephone rings_; KARL _goes to it_.
KARL.

No, ma'am, we're just going to eat now.
FRITZI.

[_Sits down beside the table._]

Blacky was a nice cat; she purred just like a steam-engine.
KARL.

Mama told you not to bring her in.
FRITZI.

Papa said I could.

[_There is the sound of footfalls._ BAUER _and his wife come in and close the door behind them_.
